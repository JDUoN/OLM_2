Welcome

This learning material explores some issues and perspectives about active citizenship and governance today at a variety of levels: local, national, European, and Global. There are various learning activities embedded in these materials. They provide those of you who are working in the community with active citizens with a framework which should help you to reflect on your own practice within these new perspectives.

Who is this learning material for?

The principal audience is a variety of citizenship educators such as: community developers, community workers, community leaders, tutors in further education colleges, youth workers, trade union tutors, and the activists who are concerned with specific contemporary issues such as: the environment, road safety, sexuality, gender, etc. Aspects of the package will also be of value to the schoolteachers. In addition, the learning material would be of value to a secondary audience such as: students of secondary schools who are doing their school projects on citizenship and active citizens themselves who have some role as educators (e.g., scout and guide leaders), local politicians, policy makers, etc.

This learning package does not require you to have any formal qualifications in order to take part in the learning activities. However, it would be desirable if you have the following background and skills:

  • a background in the community (local and/or national level) as an educator, organiser, catalyst, etc.

  • some grasp of, and interest in issues related to local and national political, social and economic aspects of the community and society

  • an appreciation of, and/or an interest in the contemporary social issues such as exclusion, migration, European integration, Globalisation, gender, the environment, etc.

  • basic skills in using a computer to browse Web pages, use email and word process simple documents. For those who are not familiar with the above, an interest and willingness to learn these skills is needed.

  • access to a computer with Internet access either at home, work, or in a public Internet access point such as an Internet caf√©, and

  • ability to read and write simple English.

What does this learning material aim to achieve?

This learning material has been designed to provide a background to the issues and perspective of active citizenship and governance today, and to provide a framework for the educators to reflect on their own practice within these new perspectives. It would enhance your understanding of contemporary debates on citizenship and conceptual shifts given the current changes and challenges facing the EU and nation states. It will introduce you to the new perspectives on learning of active citizenship and governance, and a range of innovative approaches to education/facilitations emerging from the ETGACE research.

The learning experience gained through a collaborative on-line learning environment is aimed to create a 'sense of membership' of a 'citizenship educator/facilitator community', thereby developing social networks amongst the participants. The learning experience would also create a platform for sharing information and concerns with regard to the practice of active citizenship and governance. In general, the aim of the learning material is to help the educators to become critical and better-informed practitioners who would support each other's work.

After using the learning package, we hope that you will be able to demonstrate:

  • a critical understanding of the various perspectives of active citizenship and governance

  • a critical understanding the societal changes - local, national, regional, and global, that are shaping the notion of citizenship in the 21st Century and to reflect on the implications of these on your own practice

  • an understanding of the range of barriers that prevent some sections of the society from taking part an active role in the community (gender, ethnicity, social class, economic conditions, etc.), and how these barriers operate in the society

  • an understanding of how the above barriers could be taken into account in fostering active citizenship and governance and designing educational interventions

  • a knowledge of a variety of educational interventions to foster active citizenship and governance taking into account the current changes in the societal context, and

  • the ability to improve the practice by taking into account the new perspectives on learning active citizenship

What is the content of this learning material?

This learning package is based around five themes related to active citizenship and governance. Click on each of the theme below to view an outline of the content under each of the theme. After viewing a page, click on the 'Back' button on your browser to come back to this page.

  • Theme 1 - What is Active Citizenship and Governance?
  • Theme 2 - Who is an Active Citizen?
  • Theme 3 - What forms of Active Citizenship are recognised/not recognised?
  • Theme 4 - How do people learn Active Citizenship and Governance?
  • Theme 5 - How can we facilitate learning Active Citizenship and Governance?

You will be exploring these themes through actual experiences of a number of active citizens from six countries in Europe: England, Belgium, The Netherlands, Finland, Spain, and Slovenia. We have gathered these materials as part of a pan-European research project that explored how European adults learn to become active citizens. We also explored some of these issues using experiences of people such as yourselves who are working with active citizens as educators, facilitating the development of active citizenship in various ways.

The learning material gives you an opportunity to reflect on your own life and practice. You are encouraged to contribute ideas to a Discussion Forum and interact with your fellow participants. The learning experience will enable you to develop a 'sense of membership' of a 'citizenship educator/facilitator community'.

Finally ...

Our overall objective in providing this on-line learning experience is to create a platform where you can share information and concerns about active citizenship and governance. Through the learning experience, we hope you will become more critical and better-informed practitioners. We also hope you will be more prepared to support each others' work.

We hope to modify this learning material from time to time in the light of the feedback received from users. Therefore, we invite you to use this learning material and the discussion forum and provide us with your feedback on the relevance, the userfriendliness, and any other aspects that you might consider important in modifying this online learning package.

We are also happy for other organisations to make use of this online learning package either as a whole or sections of it for educational purposes, provided that they acknowledge the source. Please email any queries to j.holford@surrey.ac.uk.
