From the Dutch life histories and from the expert meetings in the Netherlands, we can conclude that the new active citizen is seen as an adventurous type, not afraid to take risks. He is less inclined to stay long with one organisation. In fact, he is more faithful to a certain cause than to institutions or associations. The new active citizen is independent, assertive, alert and (has to) know how to deal with conflicts, with risks and with unexpected situations. He is very communicative and able to process lots of information. And finally, active citizens have relatively many and rather often changing connections with other people; they live in a diversity of networks. A very clear example in this scope is the life history of Charlotte, who clearly shows an adventurous character.

Joël van Raak and Ruud van der Veen
