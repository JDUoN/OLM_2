Active Citizens - the diversity

So who are active citizens? You have read stories of six active citizens from different countries in Europe. What do your notes say? What were your reflections based on the discussions that you have had with the fellow participants in the discussion forum? Below are our own thoughts, which you might later want to challenge based on your own analysis.

  • We can say that active citizens come from diverse backgrounds, in terms of the social class, economic position, employment, age,gender, sexuality, marital status, ethnicity, religion, skills level, etc. As our British colleague Julia Preece says: 'People who were active citizens ranged from retired professionals and middle class housewives, to unemployed young people, trade unionists and people with academic or non academic backgrounds. Active citizens were male or female, black or white.'

  • As seen in the stories you have read, and perhaps based on your experience, we also can say that active citizens take a particular interest and give personal commitment to the experiences and incidents they come across, in their own lives and/or their loved ones, and in relation to the wider society. They see the injustice, and make a personal commitment to make changes. They are not passive and want to challenge the existing authority. They have a critical awareness of society.

  • Active citizens, as we have seen in the stories, take different approaches to make these changes. Some try to do this through the existing mechanisms and structures, and by joining organisations that are already in place. Some of them realise that the existing mechanisms are not the answer, so they innovate new structures.

  • What roles do the active citizens play? In what capacities do they work? There is a variety, as you have seen in the stories, and some of them show the capacity to act in different roles: educators, organisers, fund-raisers, councillors, politicians, innovators, founders, change agents, etc.

Discussion forum

Think about the above discussion points. These suggest four ways of describing an active citizen. Can you suggest any others?
