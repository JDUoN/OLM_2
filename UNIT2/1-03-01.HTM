Peter identifies with the farmers, but did not want to join a farmers' organisation. He disliked the slowness of decision-making in social organisations and therefore runs his own 'caring' farm. Some people do not join an already existing organisation, but create one of their own. The decision to create or innovate a certain organisation or structure can be inspired by the fact that it offers opportunities to pursue one's social concerns in a personal, individual way, leaving a maximal amount of space for one's personal ambitions and sensitivities. A strong sense of autonomy (combined with solidarity for others) can be the motivating force behind active citizenship. Freedom of thought, not being dependent on others or on social structures, being true to oneself, being able to express one's opinion, and resisting social pressure are different expressions of this motive.

By taking a job in a certain sector, people grow familiar with the characteristics and the specific problems of this domain. This can enable them to take responsibility for social issues related to this sector. In this sense, work is mainly a source of informal learning. Work can open up new perspectives and forms of participation to people. Moreover it is in his job that Peter feels connected with the youngsters and acquires the necessary competencies to do something for them.

Carmen Mathijssen
