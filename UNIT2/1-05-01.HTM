Throughout the last decade, Ursa has faced a number of new challenges to which she had to react promptly and accurately. Although she has a strong professional background, she had to upgrade her knowledge from available literature and practice very quickly. To support her learning, she gained a number of friends with different backgrounds and from different countries who supported her with their knowledge and advice. Sharing information and knowledge with others in similar fields created a personal network. In this way, she created an atmosphere in which friendship, partnerships and networks could exist and develop.

On several occasions, Slovenian political parties tried to draw Ursa and the Association for Prevention under their influence. She rejected patronage both from political parties and the state. She sees political independence for NGOs and civil society initiatives as a matter of principle. In her view, political parties do not have clear enough political profiles in the 'transition states'. Therefore, she tried to have political impact through links with other national, regional and international NGOs and institutions. Links at the national level proved the most difficult. There is no doubt that the transition in Slovenia was a period of rapid change and fast learning for Ursa. As she said, she was faced with many injustices and much opportunistic behaviour, but also she came across much positive, humane action, solidarity and many exceptional people.

Angelca Ivancic, Darka Podmenik, Darijan Novak
