Mandy from England

 



Mandy's story

Mandy is a factory worker in the north of England. She is nearly 40 at the time we met her. Having left school at 16 without many academic qualifications she started working as a shop assistant. Her trade union activities began when she went to work in a factory. She was not happy that her trade union was not concerned about the welfare of the younger workers. She therefore decided to become a shop steward in order to change the culture of the trade union. In the process she learned from the courses offered by the trade union. Now she is a senior shop steward. Also she works as an occasional tutor for the trade union. Currently she is enrolled for a Masters degree in industrial relations.

Mandy described herself as a bit of a rebel at school and, as she grew older, 'a rebel without a cause' until she became involved in the trade union. She saw herself as having a strong personality and always trying to lead or organise. Her working class community background also gave her some values associated with working together, of people helping each other. So, she said she had a strong sense of 'wanting to belong' and 'encouraging people to help each other'. The union's resistance to change and lack of interest in young people gave her a cause to fight for - something she wanted to change. But Mandy also liked challenges. She regarded it as a personal challenge to get 'this old established culture' to change.

Another feature of her character was that she wanted 'to be stimulated' and wanted 'to achieve.' The trade union courses and its activities gave her a chance to 'get my brain stimulated' and she was keen to do her elected job of shop steward well: 'I am quite inquisitive and if I do something I like to know I am doing it properly … I wanted to be a good shop steward [and also] to better myself'. This combination of personal ambition and desire to achieve enabled Mandy to overcome any resistance or antagonisms towards her appointment or goals. She said 'she never felt intimidated' but alongside that her confidence grew as she gained more knowledge and skills through the courses on offer.

So for Mandy being an active citizen was about having a sense that she was growing as a person, but also having a conviction that she was able to influence something that directly affected her and people like her. For instance, she started to get involved in courses for women once she had a child because she began to realise the difficulties women have in trying to develop themselves with no child support. She needed a shared identity with the cause and she had a personality that enabled her to get involved and achieve something effectively: 'I believe people should be treated with dignity and respect.' Her trade union work enabled her to 'make sure people are treated right'.

This active citizen was energetic and had strong beliefs about the rights of her fellow human beings.

(Written by Julia Preece)
