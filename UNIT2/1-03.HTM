Peter from Belgium

 



Peter's story

Peter grew up on a farm. He did not want to become a farmer himself; instead he took up a job in a social institution that caters for youngsters with problems.

Peter was confronted with a range of experiences while working in this institution that contributed to his taking an active role, and becoming an active citizen. He became aware of how people are being treated unjustly. Improving other people's lives became his personal goal and ambition. His work with these youngsters made him aware of how difficult it is for them to find a position in society. Peter says:

Well, that was something that really startled me, that there is so much misery in so many families. Even in the families of which you would least expect it. That was my first confrontation.

After a while Peter got tired of his job because of the rigidity of its structure. He felt that the decision making processes in the organisation were very slow and against his sense of autonomy.

In the end, he went back to his father's farm and took responsibility for running it. There were other reasons for going back to his father's farm. He felt that certain things he enjoyed as a child growing up on a rural farm were being threatened and he wanted to defend those values he nurtured and grew up with. In addition, he did not agree how the media reported farming in the national press. There were also scandals related to farming. All these factors led him to take over the farm (remember, in the beginning he didn't want to have anything to do with farming!).

Peter wanted to give urban people a sense of farming as a way of life. He invited people from urban areas to get to know and value farming. As you can see, Peter felt challenged and wanted to take up a responsibility for a social commitment. After taking over his parents' farm, he converted some of it to include facilities for tourists. This was his way of making people (especially urban) aware of nature and the positive aspects of farming.

You'll remember that Peter was involved with a social organisation dealing with young people with problems, and that he left that organisation since he did not feel comfortable with it. Through the farm venture, Peter tried to combine both ambitions: farming and social work. Now he helps socially disadvantaged groups to acquire a job.

(Written by Carmen Mathijssen)
