Antonio from Spain

 



Antonio's story

Antonio has been working as a mechanic for a multinational corporation for 31 years. During his working life, he had been developing a range of activities as an independent trade unionist. His work was 'illegal' at first under the dictatorship regime of Franco. After Spain became a democracy, his work became legal.

As one of his activities in the trade union, Antonio and his fellow unionists and workers took industrial action asking for a wage increase. For this activity, he and some of his co-workers were fired from the company for three years. They however carried on their activities during these three years. A judge finally ruled in favour of them; as a result the company had to reinstate Antonio and his colleagues in their jobs.

For a time Antonio was also a town councillor in the education department in his town (Ripollet, Barcelona) as a member of a local independent coalition of social movements (COP). Through their participation in local government, they aimed to increase popular participation in local government. They campaigned for the involvement of parents in the education of children in order to improve the quality of education provision. Antonio however felt that the Mayor and his team were not really interested in listening to the demands of the parents and the public, and to boost the popular participation in decision making process. This disillusion led him (and two other COP councillors) to resign from their posts.

Antonio is critical about the mainstream unions. He has continued to be involved with other organisations within COP (the Popular Workers' Collective). At the moment, for instance, he is involved in an exchange programme between Ripollet workers and Latin American workers concerning training questions. He has not ceased to be committed to COP's activities because he believes in the importance of personal relationships among equals as an alternative to impersonal large organisations with a paternalist direction.

(Written by Jesús Gómez, Esther Oliver and Lídia Puigvert)
