Wim's story

Wim grew up in a Flemish Nationalistic family. Failure in school led to an experience of being adrift. He found a job in the army, where he appreciated the contacts with men from different regions and the cultural differences. He valued communication, and resented the aggressive approach to other people some of his fellow soldiers take. When immigration became an issue in national politics, he voted for the right wing nationalistic party to express his discontent with the democratic parties who took no stand on the matter. At the same time, (because of…), his political convictions shifted from right to left. He left the army and attained his secondary school diploma. He traveled to Guatemala and Tanzania, where he became aware of the injustice of the western life style. He got involved with Oxfam world trade, Greenpeace and similar organisations. He studied Educational sciences at university; he was interested in a method of education that respects the individuality of each child (against the levelling tendencies of traditional social institutions) and that fosters all dimensions of life (holism). He took up teaching at a school based upon the philosophy of Rudolf Steiner, and remains active in various organisations.
