Active citizens' descriptive stories - continued

Sara from Belgium

Sara is also an example of a person who is active in more than one domain of life. Here too you can look for instances where she moves from one domain to another, and one kind of activity to another, and try to identify the factors contributing to this process.



Sara's story

Sara grew up with her grandparents, who held socialist values. She has been involved in the Socialist Party but today she feels that she is more connected with the Green Party.

Sara studied ‘Social Development and Pedagogy’ in Brussels. During that time she also followed a number of courses at a non-formal adult education institution associated with culture and philosophy. These learning experiences have played an important role in her becoming an active citizen.

Professionally, Sara has been very committed to societal problems; she worked in a Youth Information Centre, a Health Care Organisation and the ‘Massereelfonds’ (a progressive cultural foundation). For Sara, work was a way of being involved as an active citizen. She gained a sense of agency and acquired skills that she was able to use later in other settings.

She was also involved actively with the Flemish Action Committee against Nuclear Weapons (in short, VAKA), organising, demonstrating and carrying out various activities. While studying at university, she also invested lot of her time as a volunteer adult literacy tutor. Another active commitment was with a women’s group against rape (‘Tegen haar wil’), to ensure a respectful treatment of rape victims. She was one of the cofounders of this group. They ran a telephone helpline, and maintained links with women's refuge, and referred women to social workers and other institutions. Sara is also active in the parents' committee of her children's school and the neighbourhood committee. She and a few others have done door-to-door research about rebuilding their street and neighbourhood, and organised meetings with local municipality to discuss the problems. Other important issues that she has taken on board include inequality in the world (striving for some kind of wealth tax/tobin tax) and the rising extremist right wing successes in elections in Belgium. She has organised and taken part in demonstrations and signing petitions to address these issues.

Sara has always taken social responsibility very personally. When she is no longer able to demonstrate such personal engagement, she searched for other domains and ways to be socially committed. For example, she stopped her engagement with the rape-victim centre because she didn’t feel qualified to cope with the heavy problems she was confronted with and to really help the women. Later she shut down the alternative café she started to organise because it was too time consuming. She quit her work in a socio-cultural association because she felt empty and burned out after eight years.

 

Reflection
