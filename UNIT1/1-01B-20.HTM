Pekka's story

Pekka was born in 1946 in Vaasa. The family was a petty bourgeois civil servant family. His father worked as a municipal secretary and was later promoted as a municipal manager. Pekka had four siblings: two older brothers, a younger sister and a younger brother. Their upbringing was quite free. However, schooling was seen as important and it was encouraged. The family was not religious, and the meaning of money was not over-emphasised. In contrast to the more or less bourgeois values of his parents Pekka later adopted a left wing world view.

In his early teenage Pekka walked in nature, liked sports, tried 4-H -club and participated in activities in the Scout Association. He especially enjoyed scout activities and attended meetings and camps quite regularly from 10 to 14 years old.

Pekka had confrontations with his teachers, and he was kept in detention after the school. He dropped out of grammar school and moved to Helsinki at 17. He had no vocational education and he had to do different odd jobs. In military service he tried to resist bullying by referring to the rulebook, but did not succeed.

After military service he trained himself as a plumber. He got married in 1968 and divorced after four years. He became a single parent for his daughter. In 1972 he was chosen as a shop steward of his work place. He started to study labour laws and regulations by himself. In addition to that, he became interested in the literature of social criticism. He was impressed, especially, by the works of Marx. In 1970s he was involved in the youth organisation of the Finnish communist party.

His employer tried to discriminate against him, and after a legal struggle in 1976-1977, he was able to hold down his job. In 1980-1981 he spent a year in Moscow studying philosophy and national/political economics. In the beginning of the 1980s he also participated in the activities of Rauhanpuolustajat (guardians of peace) organisation.

Later, he experienced a kind of crisis when the Soviet Union collapsed. Parallelly, an important reference group, the Finnish communist party broke out. In 1992 he became unemployed when his employer went bankrupt. During the same year he travelled to Latin America: Nicaragua, Mexico and Cuba.

Pekka joined in establishing an organisation for unemployed people of Helsinki in 1993. He became its chairperson. In the beginning of 2001, when the second interview was done, he worked as an executive manager of the association. During the 1990s he has participated in organising many kinds of demonstrations and campaigns. The greatest of them was called Murrostorstai (Thursday of radical change) in 1993. There were over 10 000 people demonstrating against unemployment.
