Overview

What are Active Citizenship and Governance?

What do we mean by active citizenship? And ... what do we mean by governance? Are you an active citizen? Are you involved in governance? Are there any common features which shape active citizenship and governance? Do different people agree about what these words mean? These are the issues we are going to explore in this section.

Perhaps we all can agree that words such as active citizenship and governance are difficult to define. As an active citizen, or an educator who is working with active citizens, you may have come across many people who seem to be active citizens, and many people who are involved in governance in some ways.

Let's pause for a couple of minutes ...

Reflection

Take about 2 minutes to think about the kinds of people you work with. What do they do? Do you consider what they do is active citizenship? Are they involved in governance?

Make a note of your reflections, and jot down a few key words that come to your mind.

When you have made notes about this, post these on the Discussion Forum and compare them with what your fellow participants have noted. We will come back to your and your fellow participants' reflections later on.

Learning about other active citizens and reading about what they do can be a helpful way to understand the diversity of active citizenship in our society, both within particular countries and across Europe.

The rest of the learning material contains stories of active citizens. These stories have been categorised as 'short stories' that illustrate different forms of active citizenship, and 'descriptive stories' providing more insight into particular aspects of the active citizenship of some individuals. While reading these stories you are encouraged to give some thought to the diversity of active citizenship and governance in terms of different themes and issues that matter to people, different levels and contexts in which people engage in active citizenship and the reasons for becoming active.

Happy reading!
