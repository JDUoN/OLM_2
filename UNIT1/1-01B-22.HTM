Miha's story

Miha says he has been always interested in decision making. He traces roots of this interest to his family. He realised that being catholic narrowed down the possibilities of his civic engagement and career possibilities and compromised his expectations and ambitions according to this fact. As a young man he sang in choruses and acted in amateur theatres. Later on he actively participated in a youth organisation and local community, both transmission belts of a socialist regime. During his university studies he was actively engaged in a catholic student movement. He finished his studies and became a successful middle manager.

In 1989 he contributed to the establishment of a Christian Democratic Party of Slovenia (CDP) and made a swift party career. In the first democratic elections he was elected as a member of the national parliament. As a member of the Slovenian parliamentary delegation he was lobbying in the institutions of the European Union, arguing for an independent Republic of Slovenia. He even ran for the position of the President of the Republic of Slovenia. Later on he got a position of a high state officer but was forced to resign. Afterwards he was appointed to another high level official position. During the time of holding that position he finished his Masters Degree in political sciences. Since after the termination of his office he wasn't re-elected he rejoined the ranks of politicians and took another office.
