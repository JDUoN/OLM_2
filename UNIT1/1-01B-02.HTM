Kirsi's story

Kirsi is the eldest of a family of three children. Her mother was a kindergarten teacher and her father was a proofreader. Politically, they were centre-left. Kirsi had a happy childhood. She remembers, 'the summer cottage', trips in the wild, 'yachting trips', 'Finnish Archipelago', etc. Her family had rabbits, guinea pigs and a dog. From childhood, she learned to respect other human beings and nature.

As a teenager she was influenced by Animalia -association, which distributed information on animal experiments and whaling. She became a vegetarian. She also participated in environmental campaigns and protests against whaling in Norway. In the upper secondary school she learned about Greenpeace but did not have the possibility of influencing in this professionally operated organisation. Influenced by her biology teacher, Kirsi studied subjects related to the environment.

At university Kirsi moved to a small commune near her home. She had met the other residents in the national Amnesty organisation. Through visits to other universities she became interested in the rights of sami-people. She realised that all citizens of Finland are not equal. Her insight into Finnish society and its exercise of power became deeper. She also had a feeling of being in opposition with her opinions.

In Northern-Finland she also familiarised herself with Metsä-ryhmä of Luonto-Liitto (the Forest group of the Finnish Nature League). Through practical engagement, she learned how to influence: she participated in many kinds of activities from obstructing logging to organising a forest conservation congress. Thus, many efforts of Metsä-ryhmä included more or less illegal activities. Kirsi received fines for civil disobedience.

In Metsä-ryhmä Kirsi got to know many people, including her husband. They married and moved to Joensuu in 1999. Kirsi's master thesis was accepted in 2000 with the highest score within a Finnish university system. In the beginning of 2001, she was considering a career as a researcher. She was not sure if she could influence enough through her job as an environmental educator. She is still committed with Luonto-Liitto and led its local environmental group for young persons. She also emphasised that Metsä-ryhmä would be an important spiritual context for her in the future too.
