Pamela's story

Pamela is the retired Head teacher of a school for children with disabilities and learning difficulties. She is now a local politician with responsibilities in a variety of areas such as community policy, resources, planning and health. She was born in the north of England just before the Second World war and went to many different schools because of wartime circumstances. At 18 she went to teacher training college in London, and got married soon after she began her career as a teacher. Pamela became a teacher in the special needs school, and few years later she separated from her husband, and remained in the same school until she took early retirement at the age of 55. In mid career she took a degree at London University. During this time, she became involved through a personal contact with a charity which found accommodation for young people with schizophrenia coming out of hospital. After retirement, she did not actively seek a political role but was approached and asked if she would stand for a 'hopeless seat', which she then won in 1995. She says that she 'gets a buzz from getting things done' which help people.
