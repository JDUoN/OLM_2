Alfonso's story

Alfonso comes from a family with a low socio-economic status. He has been committed to several Christian workers' associations: JOC (Christian Working Youth) and ACO (Catholic Workers' Action), as well as to a Marxist Leninist Party. Because of his illegal trade union activity during the dictatorship he had been dismissed from several companies and was also in prison for four months. His experience in prison led him to distance himself from political parties, because during those four months none of his party colleagues cared in the least for his fate. He is also critical about unions that have too vertical an organisation. He finds solidarity among workers crucial. Nowadays, he is involved in the adult education movement, as well as in an NGO connected to Venezuela.
