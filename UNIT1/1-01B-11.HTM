María's story

María was born in Tarragona. She comes from a higher middle-class family. The values of liberty and cultural interest her family conveyed to her in her childhood have played an important role in her social commitment later on in her life. At age 19 she moved to Barcelona, where she contacted the scout movement. The scout movement has deep roots in Catalonia and stood out for its important role in the fight against Franco's dictatorship. Later democratic political leaders in Catalonia received a kind of progressive training based on the latest pedagogic tendencies of the time in the scout movement. She was one of the trainers. At the age of 27 she went back to Tarragona where she contributed to the creation and development of the scout movement there. She was also connected to a pedagogic renovation movement at that historic moment. In particular, she defended the creation of a co-ed active school, both secular and Catalan. Her job in this school led her to a commitment to other themes, such as unions, the feminist movement, etc. After her fight for a truly public school succeeded, she lost her job as a teacher for she was considered not to have the required qualifications. Then she started to work at a small shop and contributed to the creation of a shopkeepers' union. She has now retired and has more time to devote to social issues and to her voluntary work with community organisation that promotes citizen participation.
