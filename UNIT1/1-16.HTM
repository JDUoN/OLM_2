A Framework identifying the multiple perspectives of Active Citizenship

Page 1 (of 2 pages)

Let's try to develop a framework to help us recognise the diversity of Active Citizenship and its multiple perspectives. At the outset we mentioned the difficulty of coming to a common agreement about the concepts such as 'active citizenship' and 'governance'. The stories that we have read so far show that active citizens are involved in a variety of activities. From these stories we can identify a number of main attributes, at least four, around which we can develop our framework, as below.

  1. Issues can be a main attribute. People become active around the issues that matter to them. For example, children's welfare was Catherine's main concern. There were others who were concerned with more than one issue.

  2. Sphere of influence, or the geographical proximity of activity. While some people tend to limit their activities to one level (Martha's activities tend to focus on the local political arena), others are active at a variety of levels. For example, Catherine's activity started from family level. Now she is active in her local area.

  3. Role. Individuals are active in a variety of roles, as you have seen in their stories. Some are active in more than one role or capacity. For example, Sandra is a founder of a new organisation to empower people. She is also a fund-raiser.

  4. Domains of life could be the fourth category: private, civil society, work, and state.

Can you think of other ways of describing the diversity of active citizenship?
