Active Citizens - Descriptive Stories

Now we will read some descriptive stories of few individuals who are active in variety of domains.

Jaap from the Netherlands

First let's read the story of Jaap, an active citizen from The Netherlands. This story illustrates a person who is active in more than one domain in life. While you read the story, look for instances where he moves from one domain to another, and one kind of activity to another, and try to identify the factors contributing to this process.



Jaap's story

Jaap was born in a Catholic family of 14 children. As the youngest child, he had the privilege of going to school beyond primary level. The others had to work in their father's cigar factory soon after they completed primary schooling. After Junior High School, Jaap started to work at the Post Office, which he didn't like. The early career was an unsatisfying and blunt period for him. Working at the Post Office was too sultry and restrictive for him. He says: 'selling stamps, counting money, all that nonsense. It was no challenge to me.' He was among colleagues who were 'not interested in society', and 'they never ever read a book.'

Jaap moved on, and later on became an Executive in a large company. There, he experiences injustice and very negative power relations. He explains the situation: 'For example all engineers automatically became directors. I tried to break that automatism and abuse of power.' His attitudes and values lead to a conflict with his superiors. Consequently he was dismissed from his duties. While being bitter about this experience, Jaap feels proud about his integrity and being able to maintain his values: 'I have always been faithful to my own beliefs, [although] at that time, it ruined my career.'

Jaap also became a director of the Catholic trade union for governmental employers at the age of 24. Most of his family were involved in various types of associations and societies. After five years however, he decided to leave the trade union. The values of the union conflicted with his own values and attitude too much. Jaap explains: 'At the end, I did not feel comfortable in the trade union anymore. Most people were very biased and restricted, and could be rather hard as well. In the union I felt like an analphabetic.'

Education and personal development have always been important to Jaap. To fulfill his hunger for social, cultural and political knowledge, Jaap enrolled at the University of Leiden to study Sociology . This was one of the best periods of life; he enjoyed the sense of personal growth and development.

After completing studies, Jaap became active in the political domain. He thought that politics can contribute to solving social problems: '[After studying Sociology] I realized there were lots of blockades in society. I wanted to change the situation of inequality and injustice. I thought by being politically active, I could solve these problems.'

Soon Jaap encountered difficulties with party politics. He switched from a conservative party to a progressive political party. The switching of political parties shows his search for integrity and professionalism. He says: "Politics is rather hard. Ending up in that circuit, you have to realize that you will get hurt. You always have to be on your guard. I am branded by the machinery of politics and government. Many people are hypocrites and bogus. I am not like that. Honesty and integrity are very important standards for me.'

Besides his work and his activities in politics, Jaap has also been active in the civil society. 'People knew me from my activities in politics. Because I had good insight in government, they asked me to contribute to different kind of interest groups.' Jaap has been very active for the Catholic Church. He is active in committees, counsels, foundations, and study groups, in a number of capacities. 'The Evangel has always been very important for me. In the Evangel as well as in my life, solidarity is an essential topic.' He has been a member of numerous Boards: as a commissioner in a housing association, a member for a domestic science school, a member of his children's Montessori school, and an advisory member in the social provision of services.

Since his retirement, Jaap especially invests his time in interest groups for elderly. He has been active as a secretary for an elderly association, as the chairman of the study group 'policy for elderly', as a member of the committee 'Day of the Elderly', etc. Jaap wants to be important for the elderly people as long as possible. Therefore, he tries to keep up with new developments in the ever-changing world by reading world literature and actively participating to all kinds of social organisations.

Jaap takes an active caring role in his family too. He has a some with schizophrenia and his wife is suffering from dementia. Jaap feels very responsible for them and it is a lot of effort to combine his social and political activities with the care for his family. Jaap feels a very strong desire to stay active, despite the problematic situation in the private domain. In order to succeed in this, a very strict planning is inevitable.

 

Reflection

  • What words would you use to describe Jaap's active citizenship?
  • What areas of life was he active in? What were the reasons for being active in these different areas of life?
  • What did he do in different areas of life?
