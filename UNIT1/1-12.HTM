Active citizens' descriptive stories - continued

Rosalía from Spain



Rosalía's story

Rosalía has been engaged with social movements from a very young age. She felt her initial period of social commitment was a momentous one. Immediately after Franco's death she became involved in youth associations, women's associations and cultural associations.

Currently, her participation in social movements has decreased and she says she now focuses on fewer issues than those in which she is really interested. She once participated in a student exchange programme between Spain and Nicaragua through an international solidarity NGO, as she believes it is important to provide people from the Third World with an opportunity to study. She is still involved in this NGO, but at a much lower level of involvement.

She works at a vocational training institute, and she feels committed to her job. Her work targets young people of different profiles, among them, young school dropouts. She thinks that with these training programmes these people can have deeper insight into the world of work and they can provide them with tools that can help them for later entering the labour market.

 

Reflection

  • What are the different areas of life in which Rosalía has been active? What different issues does she take on board as an active citizen?
  • To what extent has her active citizenship been influenced by the political climate in which she grew up? Which other stories provide examples of similar experiences?
  • Rosalía's activities stretch to the global level as well. Have you come across active citizens who take part in activities that go beyond their local and national boundaries? What makes people global citizens?
