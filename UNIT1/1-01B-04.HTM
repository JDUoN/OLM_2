Ana's story

Ana comes from a small town in the Slovenian countryside where she has been living since she was a child. Her parents were public servants. She has a family with two school children. During her growing up and her university study she became concerned with the protection of nature and preservation of traditional culture, and also developed resistance to the state centralism in relation to the regions. When democratic changes in Slovenia enabled political pluralism she joined a newly established political party in her region. She was quickly promoted in the hierarchy of the party. In 1996 she was elected to the national parliament. During her mandate she successfully participated in several regional developmental projects. In addition to that she also extended her activities to some social issues. Among others she participated in the parliamentary commission for equal opportunities. Now, she is a vice mayor of the commune where she lives.
