Active citizens' descriptive stories - continued

Ana from Slovenia

Ana's story shows how a particular context produces a certain type of active citizenship. She was involved in typical traditional politics during the period when Slovenia underwent a transition towards democracy.



Ana's story

Ana lives in a small town in the countryside of Slovenia, where she was born and grew up. Her parents are government servants. She has two young children.

Ana is among the key people in a regional branch of one of the political parties newly established in the transition from socialist to democratic system. She has been an elected MP from 1996 - 2000.

As a student Ana participated in various cultural groups and events in her local community. But she was never interested either in political activities or in any other organised group activities in the public sphere, not even the Students' Union. When transition processes took place, she had just finished her studies and began to work.

Ana took over a part of the firm where she was working, thanks to the economic and organisational restructuring of firms and enterprises. As a young entrepreneur she began to be interested in social and economic changes taking place in Slovenia. She felt that there were some changes going on in different parts of Slovenia while in the region where she lived no changes were likely to occur. Her particular concern was strong centralisation which did not make it possible for peripheral parts of Slovenia to develop. Preservation of cultural heritage and tradition and the development of the rural parts of Slovenia were also her interest. She was realistic that complaining to friends did not make any difference. She says::

When I took a job, the things in our environment, this political activity and this constant presence of the same people, started to bother me. To me it seemed as if things in L., or in any other place, went forward, here they stayed the same, connected to the same people, as if they were asleep. And I said to myself, 'well, yeah, there needs to be some change here'. But you can only change something if you yourself are active in… All these things happening in the pubs are not productive, are they?

Since her goals and expectations were similar to those of one of the newly established political parties she accepted the invitation of her friends to join the party. Shortly after becoming a member she also ran for more responsible positions in the party. Political activities took most of her time, and she decided to give up her work as an entrepreneur.

In 1996-2000 she was elected an MP. As an MP she co-ordinated some projects in her region launched by the government which aimed for the economic restructuring of the region. She was also a chairman of the parliamentary commission on equal opportunities which dealt with various social policy issues. She used her position as an MP and a member of the political party which was in the government coalition to pursue some issues concerning gender inequalities. She was especially active in a group which was preparing new measures to enable women to get access to political positions, although at the end the new proposal was not passed by the parliament. Moreover, she was involved in some parliamentary activities to raise awareness about, and to bring in new measures to prevent, the sexual abuse of women.
