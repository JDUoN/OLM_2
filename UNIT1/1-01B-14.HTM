Leo's story

Leo's father was a farmer and mother was a trainee agriculturist. The parents worked assiduously and saved up for a house. They moved when Leo was about 7 years old. Leo described the set of values of the family by saying that for economic reasons his parents preferred to give up buying milk rather than to resign from the book club. The economic situation of the family improved when the father succeeded as an entrepreneur. The social respect of the family increased especially, because his father bought a large forest tract in the middle of the best deer-stalking area.

The locals were wondering about the son of a farmhand who succeeded in a school without reading. In the school he was bullied and developed anorexia in the summer just before the upper level of comprehensive school. He went to the upper secondary school of Kotka lyceum mostly because his best friends also went there. It was there, where Leo became interested in information technology. After secondary school graduation he entered the Faculty of Electrical and Communications Engineering at a university. However, he did not feel at home in this faculty and transferred to the faculty of Industrial Management and started to study industrial psychology. According to him: "humans seemed much more interesting and complex subjects to program than computers". The decision paid off and Leo got his master's thesis in 1995.

Leo told his life story as a human, as a writer, as a politician and as a researcher. As a human: he fell in love in a confirmation class, he became engaged to his childhood friend in 1980 when the couple were entering to upper secondary school and he married his fiancée in 1989. The first child was born in 1994.

Leo has always expressed himself through writing. He wrote fairy tales as early as pre-school. Writing was his favourite subject in school too. The first published stories appeared during the period of the upper secondary school. The first story was published in the local paper, Kotkansilmä. It described how Leo's class had organised the fund-raising for development aid. Another published article in the Prosessori -journal was concerned with programming a computer game. Later he has written dozens of articles and books about home computers. He has also published a couple of science fiction short stories and a radio programme series. In addition, he had spent a couple of years as the editor-in-chief of Kosmoskynä, the journal of the Finnish Science Fiction Writers' Association.

Leo entered politics in late 1994, as his conscience did not allow him to continue complaining without offering an alternative. This alternative presented itself as a new party, the Young Finns Party. He collected 5000 names in order to establish a new political party. He also took part in the 1995 parliamentary election and carried out his election campaign on the Internet. In the 1996 double election his 1185 votes were not enough to get him to the EU-parliament, but he succeeded in the council election and become a councillor and a member of the city board too. In the 1999 parliamentary elections no candidates of the Young Finns Party were elected to the parliament and its members decided to dismantle the whole party. After that Leo found a new political home in the Green League. In the year 2000 municipal elections he became re-elected to the city council from the Green League list.

Leo never left his old university and he has been working in the Laboratory of Industrial Psychology and Leadership since 1990, first as a research assistant, then as a researcher. In 1995 he started working on his postgraduate studies. His studies have been connected with electronic performance support systems in learning organizations. He participates in public discussions in the role of researcher too. Parallelly, the work gives him tools for influencing. He likes his job but is not planning to retire as a researcher.
