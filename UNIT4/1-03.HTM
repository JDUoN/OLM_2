Learning to become an active citizen - some key points

In Theme 4 we attempted to explore the main question of how people learn to become active citizens. In particular we wanted to look at the nature and the main features of the learning process, and the factors that contribute to it. And finally we want to see what we can do, as active citizens, educators, and facilitators, to strengthen this learning process.

These are difficult questions; we know the difficulty of making generalisations about the learning process or the factors that contribute to the learning process. The life stories in this learning material, specifically those of Beryl and Jeffrey, and your own experience show this complexity and the diversity.

And .. we need your help here! We only can observe some tendencies and patterns, which we have summarised in the checkered box on the next page. We have left the blank 'question mark' boxes for you to fill in based on your own analysis of the stories that you have read, and your own experience with active citizens. The observations made on the gender issue is based on women's stories that appeared in Theme 1.
