Beryl's story

1: Values and attitudes fostered during early socialisation

Beryl talked about how her family, close friends, religion, and cultural affiliations nurtured values and attitudes in childhood:

The main influence for me was my family and culture; the family that I grew up in, and my immediate family and the links that I made, I think that is very important.

Coming from a culture and a tradition which fosters extended families, she strongly believes in the social capital built around such an extended family, friends and relatives. In line with her values, she is involved in a number of community organisations and activities. For example, she is a member of a council concerned with the welfare of the ethnic minority community.

The influence of religion is reflected in her choice of a career when she was about 20. She chose to become a nurse, and the reason was:

I was always inclined to do something that was, I would be naïve if I said Godly, but was brought up as a Christian, and I always had those tendencies and when I was quite young I wanted to be a missionary and [later] I wanted to be a nurse.

She mentions that 'to be a Christian' is 'very important to [her]'. She was 'brought up as a Christian and she tries to 'live a Christian life'. She says she always 'believed in healing' and her recovery from the life-threatening illness (described later) has brought the religion 'closer to home.'
