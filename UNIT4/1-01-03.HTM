Beryl's story

3: A critical awareness of the society

Beryl's mentioned a coming of age in her life, and a time before that. She explains that the totality of her learning experiences were helpful for her to develop an awareness of the society she lives in:

It [the totality of learning experiences] brought more awareness: awareness of community and living, dealing with domestic violence, women's issues, race issues, class issues, ... ..

She says the significance of these learning experiences:

brought up a lot of awareness that I didn't have before, because don't forget that I came here at 19 and before that I was sheltered and protected and I had no educational exposure to certain things and … although it was late I would probably be 30 by [that time], this was like me coming of age.

The coming of age is the development of a critical awareness of the society she lives in. She says that she was becoming 'more aware of other things and again this is the start of [her] community work.' Interacting with other women who had similar experiences was key to this development of the critical awareness of the society she lives in. Her work with an organisation related to ethnic minority groups was helpful for her to understand the racial issues in the society.
