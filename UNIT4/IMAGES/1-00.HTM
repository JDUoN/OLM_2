Overview

How do people learn Active Citizenship and Governance?

Active citizenship implies individual and/or collective acts of social responsibility, towards others/on behalf of others. Active citizenship is also linked to the notion of governance, i.e., participation and involvement in the decision making processes that affects wider society. Our main question in this theme is how do people learn to take this kind of social responsibility. In your work you must have come across many active citizens, contributing in various ways to their communities and their families. Have you ever thought how do they become active citizens? What are the factors that contribute to that transformation? You may have lot to say about this through your own life and your work with active citizens.

We will explore these issues through stories of two people we've met, Beryl from England and Jeffrey from Belgium. Through Beryl's story, we'll attempt to identify the main factors that contributed to her learning. We also will try to exammine the main features of the learning process. Through Jeffrey's story, we'll try to identify three main conditions that help the learning process: challenge, capacity, and connections. Based on these two stories, and the other stories that you have read so far in other Themes, and your own reflections as you read the stories, we'll attmpt to identify some commonalities in relation to learn activecsitizenship. Finally we ask the question how can we facilitate the learning of active citizenship, which will be the main focus of the next theme.

Reflection

Think of your own life story.

  • How did you become an active citizen?
  • How did you learn to do what you are doing now?
  • Are there any factors that you can identify as contributed to your becoming an active citizen? Can you characterise your own learning process?
