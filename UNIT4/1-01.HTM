Beryl's story

Beryl was 56 at the time she told her story. She migrated to England from Jamaica when she was 19. She faced many difficulties as a black, single mother, without much prospect of a decent living. She however had the courage and the determination to study.

She trained to become a nurse, and went to the local college for night classes. She became aware of the issues facing people like herself, and started to work in the voluntary sector. At 40 she was diagnosed as having cancer, and just as she was recovering, her son met with a serious accident. Among other things, she founded a self-help organisation for women for which she was recently awarded the first 'unsung women's award.'

There are many factors that contributed to her becoming an active citizen, which, for now, we categorised as follows. You can click on each of the categories to read her experiences.

  • Values and attitudes fostered during early socialisation
  • Personal circumstances and critical incidents
  • A critical awareness of the society
