There are forms of active participation that are not recognised because they do not follow 'established' avenues. A clear example is the Gypsy community in Spain. Gypsy people are considered to be neither interested in politics nor active participators. This is because political institutions exclude Gypsy culture, and not because it is not participatory.

The Gypsies are very active and participatory inside their own subculture, but their participation is not recognised by the wider Spanish society. The women, who are active in the less visible private domain, find their contribution to building society especially unappreciated. In this way, their active citizenship is limited and obstructed by society's view of them.

Carmen Mathijssen
