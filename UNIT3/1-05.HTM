De Wrikker in Belgium

 



De Wrikker's Story

Economic conditions and work organisations provide a context in which active citizenship is practised. The opportunities which the world of work can offer may be reduced or even destroyed by changes to the labour market. Demands for increased flexibility, productivity and efficiency make it harder for individuals to be active citizens in or through work. And in many low-skilled occupations, the content of work contains little challenge or opportunity for fulfillment.

De Wrikker is an exception.

De Wrikker is a printers' collective started in 1975 as a reaction against hierarchy and authority at the work place. In the post 1968 period, lots of action groups and organisations needed cheap printing. Like many other organisations developed in that period, De Wrikker wanted to work more horizontally, in a participatory way. De Wrikker is nonhierarchical, and all decisions are made in a participatory way: everyone is involved in the decision-making process.

De Wrikker still maintains its unique approach to work; it is a little island working completely differently from other Belgian companies. De Wrikker employees' work in the printing office gives meaning to their activity. The working conditions are adapted to the people who work there. The business is kept deliberately on a small scale.

Although all over the world working people's circumstances and experiences differ, their situations result from similar mechanisms. Making the different but interwoven consequences of global economic changes visible can be a basis for connections between people. Alternative conceptions, practices and organisations of work should be encouraged, not only as alternatives for individuals, but also as ways to change organisations and companies.

An organisation like De Wrikker can take up an 'active' role, making choices about production, what is produced, to whom it is sold, and at what price.

(Written by Carmen Mathijssen)
