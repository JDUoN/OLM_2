Mojca from Slovenia

 



Mojca's Story

Mojca was born shortly after the Second World War in a rural village in Slovenia. She was brought up in a Catholic family with strong Catholic values. As a child, she was brought up to take care of family responsibilities, and already in her childhood she felt the need for spiritual growth. The priest was an influential figure in her upbringing.

She always wanted to be a teacher. After completing school education, she went to a secondary school for teachers. However she was not able to complete the teacher training course. Her family was not financially strong enough to support her schooling so she had to go to work. While working she studied part-time, and completed 4 years of secondary education.

Later she got married, and started a large family with five children. She describes her family and professional life:

'…we had five children, all have a good education… When I was employed, it was just work and family, I could not afford any additional activities, no meetings, no friends, just work [and family] … .

Her work and her family completely absorbed her time, and there was no space left for any activities outside her home or her office. She however found some time to become active in a trade union, and to be a mentor for a number of interns at work.

The death of her husband marked an important turning point in her life. First she experienced a breakdown. Her family and the colleagues at work helped her to come through this difficult period of her life. She learned that there are other people experiencing similar situations and problems. She started to reach out to others who were in need and to provide help and support.

After her children were grown up, she had more time to help others. She retired from her paid work, and became engaged in organised public activities outside the family and work sphere. Now she is active in a local society for the retired. Within this society she has organised a club for lonely old people where they can enjoy company of others. She reflects on her work:

When I've seen that I'm useful to people, I realised I enrich myself the most.

Mojca also became involved in political activities. She became a member of a leftist party; she wanted to oppose the growth of the capitalist trend in the Slovenian society. She thought she should:

... fight for the workers, the equity, equality of people … .

She also writes articles for newspapers and magazines where she expresses her opinion on actual social conditions. Recently she received an award for her exceptional work in the public sphere.

(Written by Angelca Ivancic, Darka Podmenik, Darijan Novak)
