Marlene's early, private experiences were now being used for public gain in the form of active participatory support alongside the activities of other women.

Marlene's story shows that the forms of activity that are recognised as active citizenship are ones that have some kind of public, collective role and ones that reach out to others. It may be that Marlene's relatively passive support (letting women come to visit the centre in order to give counselling support) would not be regarded as active citizenship compared with more publicly recognised organisational roles. Nevertheless there are distinctions between her early passive and reactive life to the current, proactive role she is playing in the women's centre.

Julia Preece
