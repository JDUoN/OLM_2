Jukka's biography illustrates a person who has been excluded from mainstream schooling due to his disability. Jukka not only lived to prove that the doctors' predictions were wrong, but also to prove that he could fight back and be involved and change the decision making processes in society, some of which were responsible for excluding him from schooling. Jukka was about 30 years when he joined the local association of disabled people, promoting social equality. Parallelly, he became interested in municipal politics but experienced that he could not become fully recognised, because of his insufficient education. So, he restarted his schooling and proceeded step by step. In January 2001, he was studying public law at the University of Lapland.

Jukka believes that his own private life provides a rich source of experiences which he can draw on in his public life, in making decisions about others lives. His own life was a battle since he was 7 years old, with the disability, and having being excluded from regular school for his disability. One of his mottoes is that before one is ready to influence other people's affairs, s/he has to make order in his/her own affairs. Most of the impossible looking things can be arranged with the help of proper information. And in his opinion proper information never comes by itself (at least not in time), you have to apply/to look for it.

Matti Laitinen & Kari E. Nurmi
