Jukka from Finland

 



Jukka's Story

Jukka was born in 1958 in a rural part of northern Finland. His father was a machine operator in a road construction company, and mother, a housewife. He had a brother and a sister. Jukka's childhood was a 'normal ordinary childhood', at least until he was seven years old.

At the age of seven, Jukka noticed that his vision was deteriorating. He couldn't see in the twilight. (Later he becomes almost blind). Because of his bad eyesight he was not able to perform well in his class, in spite of his parents' efforts. Gradually it became difficult for him to get into the school bus.

During this time, Jukka felt that he was always the slowest in the physical training lessons. Doctors found that Jukka has a progressive muscle deterioration disease. Their prediction was that Jukka will have some four or five years to live, at the most. Doctors were wrong, and four years later, Jukka started to use a wheel chair to support his mobility.

Local authorities wanted Jukka to study at a special school for children in a place far away from his home. Jukka refused go there; he did not want to leave home and be with strangers in a strange place. He was 13 at that time. Although he insisted on getting personal support, he was not successful. His parents were not able to change the decision of the authorities. They also did not try to persuade Jukka to attend the school for disabled children, which was located far away from his home. In the end, he was 'freed from schooling obligations.'

Later on, his brother also developed a muscle deterioration disease, but his condition developed much slower than that of Jukka. His brother managed to complete his school normally.

Since about 12, Jukka was interested in social issues. He followed what was on the radio, press and television about politics. Jukka had few friends with whom he was able to exchange views on society and politics.

When he was about 30, he wanted to be actively involved in society. He wants to influence the decision making process. He wanted to be a member of the local city council. However he felt that he wasn't prepared adequately for this. He had only six years of formal education. He was determined to get formal qualifications.

Well past the formal schooling age, and with two forms of disabling factors, Jukka had a big task ahead. It was a challenge to find a place to study, and means to pay for it. Eventually he found financial support for completing his secondary education. He followed a course of study that suited his needs and disability.

Jukka completed the high school programme in two years with very good results. While studying the high school programme, he moved out of his parents' house. He moved into a purpose-built accommodation for the disabled. He also joined the local associations of the disabled, which aims to protect the interests of the local disabled people. In that period he starts to express his opinions in the local press as well.

After completing secondary education, Jukka decided to go on to the university. His friends told him about the Finnish Open University system whereby one can study in a normal university as a regular student after getting sixty credits from the Open University. Once again Jukka has to go through many obstacles to finance his studies in the Open University. He completed the 60 credits of the Public Administration Studies, and became a regular student of another university. Public Law was his main subject.

At this point he decided that he was not weak or disabled anymore. Education played a major role in changing attitudes towards his own self. He felt comfortable to join the local council, and made his first attempt. He became an associate member of the local parliament and a regular member of the local community administration. Jukka became a member of the city parliament, at the age of 42, in his third attempt at the elections. He also continues his work with the Department of the Social and Health Affairs and in the associations of the disabled.

During this time Jukka almost died because of his breathing muscles retardation. It took a lot of spiritual and physical efforts to survive. He started to use a breathing machine, approximately 15 hours a day, to support his breathing.

(Written by Matti Laitinen & Kari E. Nurmi)
