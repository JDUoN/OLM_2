Study Circles (Finland)

This is an example of the use of study circles as a approach to learning within a young farmers' organisation called Alkuvoima (literally translated to English as Primal Force).



Alkuvoima was born in 1993 among young farmers in South-East Finland. Its aim was to maintain the Finnish countryside as a viable and enjoyable environment, and to improve the image and position of farmers. The young farmers felt that they were treated in unfair ways in many contexts such as in MTK (the Central Union of Agricultural Producers and Forest Owners) and in the Finnish media. For this reason, they formed a group to improve the image of farmers and other country people.

Anja, one of the founding members of Alkuvoima explains the functions of the organisation:

We established Alkuvoima (Primal Force) group because we young [farmers] never succeeded in achieving positions of influence (in MTK). Our way of behaving was too visible, critical and radical. They did not trust us. So we too decided to act alone. Finland was then about to join the European Union - there was a very sharp antagonism between country and town. Some young women were in a similar situation. They had also experienced the loneliness of being mothers of young children, and the lack of 'soul mates' in their neighbourhoods. Then men started to join our group too. It began as an informal group, and very quickly became a kind of study circle. We supported each other, and it also became a protest group, capable of reacting very fast.

Alkuvoima has about 40 members. Most are young farmers, but there are also a baker, a diplomat, a headmaster, and a university scientist among others. There is no age limit and both women and men are welcome. Alkuvoima has no formal organisational hierarchy, but an organic nucleus of seven women has run most of its activities. The group aims to change the world by disseminating information about the views of young farmers. The group has written press notices, arranged street campaigns and demonstrations, met politicians and contacted various organisations.

Study circles perform an important role in the learning process of the organisation, as Anja explains:

We established two study circles in 1994. The first was Euroopan kasvot and the second Face of Europe (The names have the same meaning, one in Finnish, the other in English.) Euroopan kasvot studied how the European Union is functioning and what opportunities citizens have to influence it. Face of Europe began by studying English, agricultural English, and developed contacts with farmers in the European Union. I suggested: "Let's send friendly letters to mid-European agricultural journals. We can ask them to publish our pen-friend advertisements for free: we want to correspond with colleagues. And perhaps they can send us some material." We sent our advertisements to eight journals in England, Scotland, Wales, Ireland, Belgium, France, Germany and Austria. We received over a hundred responses.

Networking also plays an important part in Alkuvoima's learning process.
