Networking and Learning (The Netherlands)

This is a network-based approach to strengthening active citizenship. This particular story is about Echte welvaart ('Real Prosperity'), a network of nearly thirty associations and institutions involved in a variety of activities and initiatives, initiated by the green movement and supported by churches, charities and humanist groups.



Echte welvaart aims to promote values in our society such as equality, solidarity, empathy and care. The goal is to achieve a better balance between economic, ecological and social values by stimulating thought and provoking a public debate about the dominant ideology of economic growth and consumerism. A member of the Echte welvaart explains how they work and what they do:

We want to stimulate professionals and active members of associations within the green movement, and members of other social and religious organisations, to reflect on their role and position and on the way they bring about these values. Changes in society towards sustainability and a higher quality of life require that people take into account the interests of others now and in the future. In this campaign, we use values and concepts people accept as important. So we do not impose anything upon people. We try to trigger discussions about the values people share at work, in school, at home and in associations and churches. We "mirror" these values and ask people: "So what can you do about it?".

The Echte welvaart network adopts, supports and links many different activities and projects. People share knowledge and inspire each other. They share common values and goals, without losing their own identity. Such a learning network creates a diverse and rich coalition, in which people and initiatives are strengthened. The network consists of about 500 local projects, clustered by province and target group. Examples of participating partners are Union of Nurses and Guardians, the Consumer Union, Animal Protection, National Centre Welfare Work, Dutch Women's Council, Council of Churches, and Organisation for Refugees.

The Echte welvaart has three main functions: to do, to learn and to show. In the activity 'to do', the network adopts and strengthens certain projects. There is strong cooperation between partners, experts, regional and local opinion leaders and decision-makers. In the activity 'to learn', the projects become laboratories for Echte welvaart. In cooperation with scientists and practitioners, changing mechanisms are studied through workshops and master classes. Finally, in the activity 'to show', the findings are published. The campaign's slogan is 'Seeing is believing'. The network publishes articles and essays in magazines and newspapers, contributes to television programmes and has its own web site.

In addition, the network brings professionals, experts and decision-makers together by organising master classes, where they discuss strategies and methods to implement these values in their work. The main goals of the master classes include:

  • To reflect on the values of the organisation, how they correspond to the values of Echte welvaart, and how these values find expression in society
  • To inspire action in their own organisations and explore how to make contact with their target groups
  • To consider and manage resistance from individual group members and change agents
  • To integrate certain values within the goals of their organisations
  • To gain insight into social interests that embrace or hinder the values of Echte welvaart.

An important factor when considering the master class method is how to bring together three learning levels (individual, organisation, and society) using different working methods (gathering information, reflecting on it and putting it into practice).

(Written by Joël Van Raak)
