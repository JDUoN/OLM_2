Community Activities and Leadership Development in Young People

There are a number of programmes that train young people in community activities and leadership, such as those offered by the Civilia Foundation . The Civilia Foundation develops Common Purpose programmes outside the UK and is 'sister body' to Common Purpose UK. The aim of Common Purpose is to educate 'men and women from a broad range of geographical, political, ethnic, institutional, social and economic backgrounds' on 'constitutional, civic, economic, and social studies with special emphasis on civil and social awareness and responsibility in the United Kingdom and elsewhere' . In addition to programmes in the UK, where over 70 different programmes are run, the Civilia Foundation has helped to develop programmes in Ireland, Germany, Sweden, and South Africa.

Organisations outside the UK interested in having a Common Purpose programme in their cities would become a licensee partner and the staff of Civilia Foundation would work with them in developing programmes. Local staff lead the programmes in each country, and Civilia staff assist with fund raising, recruitment, programme design, and the overall establishment of the programme. The local staff in each country have access to the Common Purpose UK knowledge base, manuals, software, and curriculum resources, which they can adopt according to the local conditions and needs.

There are over 12,000 'graduates' of Common Purpose programmes in the UK and elsewhere. Common Purpose runs a range of programmes, such as

    • Matrix and Focus - the original city based programme for leaders and decision makers from the public, private and not-for-profits sectors (average age 38)
    • Navigator - for people under 30 starting out on their careers - again taking a mix of people from the public, private and not-for-profit sectors, and
    • Your Turn - for 14 year olds taken from a cross-sections of schools in their cities.

Common Purpose programmes aim to increase the number of informed individuals who are actively involved in shaping the future of the area in which they live and work. These programmes use a range of methods to suit the different ways people learn: both by experience and through structured sessions. The Internet is used extensively in helping to provide participants with information on the issues covered by the programme. Common Purpose has recently launched the UK's largest Website for active citizens, called citizensconnection.net and a Website for young citizens yourturn.net , as part of the mechanisms for learning active citizenship and democracy.
